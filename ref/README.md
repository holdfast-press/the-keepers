# The Keepers — Series Reference Foundation

This folder contains the canonical reference documents for *The Keepers* series. These are the definitive sources for all creative decisions. In any conflict between these documents and the working manuscript, **these documents take precedence**.

All files here are deduplicated — only the latest approved version of each document is stored. Version history is preserved in git.

---

## Documents

### `outline.md`
**The master story outline for Book One.**
Contains the series concept and hook, Hal's 15-year friendship timeline ("The Earned Years"), plot mechanics of how the characters are drawn together, the full 24-chapter structure across 3 parts, and the dual-timeline mechanics (present day and Norse past).

*Use when:* planning scenes, placing chapters, checking the arc of any character relationship.

### `character-bible.md`
**Complete character profiles for all major characters.**
Each character has a modern identity, a Norse past-life identity, their relationship to Hal, and their role in the central mystery. Includes the core MVP group and secondary characters.

*Use when:* writing any character — their names, speech patterns, backstory, and Norse identity must match this document.

### `prologue.md`
**The series prologue.**
The narrative opening that precedes Chapter One. Sets the mythic register and introduces the Norse framing before the present-day story begins.

*Use when:* editing or placing prologue material.

### `chapter-01.md`
**First chapter draft.**
The narrative opening at a Microsoft Ignite conference in San Francisco. Introduces Hal Bergstrom and the present-day world.

*Use when:* editing Chapter One or checking narrative voice for subsequent chapters.

### `branding.md`
**Series branding guide.**
Tone, themes, emotional register, visual identity notes, and the series tagline (*What we choose to carry, carries us*). Covers the three-author voice blend (Lewis/Tolkien/Lawhead) and how the modern/mythic registers should coexist.

*Use when:* writing marketing copy, series descriptions, or calibrating voice.

### `series-outline.md`
**Multi-book series arc.**
The broader shape of the series beyond Book One: thematic arcs, character trajectories, the long answer to the central mystery, and how subsequent books develop.

*Use when:* making decisions that have implications beyond Book One, or planning long-range character development.

## Rules for this folder

- Do not add new documents without author approval
- Do not modify documents without noting the change and the reason
- Version control is the history — do not add version numbers to filenames
- Any proposed change to canon must be flagged to the author before committing
