# THE KEEPERS: WHAT THE STONES REMEMBER
## *Book One of The Keepers*
## Character Bible — v0.6

---

## Tone & Content Notes

Adult fiction (R-rated equivalent). Real language, honest violence, real intimacy when the story earns it, unflinching grief.

## The Microsoft MVP Through-Line

The crew is bound in modern times by the **Microsoft MVP Award.** The annual **MVP Global Summit** in Redmond, Washington is where they first met years ago. The story slowly reveals that the MVP program didn't *create* their connection — it just gave them rooms in which to find each other again.

## Eikvik — Setting *(Locked)*

A small fjord-side settlement in **Trøndelag, central Norway**, off a side branch of what would much later be called Trondheimsfjord. **Late 9th to early 10th century CE.** The era of Harald Fairhair. Predates the founding of Nidaros by a generation. The old gods are still the gods. Norse-Gaelic trade with the Hebrides is established.

## Hal Bergstrom — *Halvard, son of Eirik Greybeard*

> *"He keeps going to Ignite every year because it's the conference Beth knew him as attending."*

### Modern Life

**Full name:** Harold "Hal" Bergstrom
**Age:** 43
**Lives:** Mueller neighborhood, Austin, Texas. Three-bedroom craftsman bungalow he can't bring himself to sell.
**Origin:** Born and raised in Edina, Minnesota. Father's family came from Norway in 1908 and Anglicized "Halvard" to "Harold" at Ellis Island. He has been "Hal" since he could speak. Moved to Austin in his late twenties for a job he ended up keeping.
**Work:** Principal Cloud Architect at a major consulting firm. **Azure MVP for the past nine years.** Speaks at Ignite, MMS MOA, Workplace Ninjas, Experts Live US/EU, and the MVP Global Summit. Four to six conferences a year.
**Marital status:** Widower. Has never been divorced.

**The Tragedy.** Four years before the story opens, Hal's wife **Beth** (an ER nurse, his college sweetheart) and their seven-year-old daughter **Maya** were killed in a head-on collision on **I-35** driving home from **Austin-Bergstrom International** to pick him up after **Microsoft Ignite**. A drunk driver crossed the median. Hal's flight had landed twenty minutes earlier. He was sitting at baggage claim with a stuffed shark for Maya in his carry-on when the state trooper found him.

He never used the stuffed shark. It is in Maya's room, on her pillow. Maya's room is unchanged.

**The drunk driver was not random.** Brand engineered the accident. Hal does not learn this until Chapter 16.

**Ignite is the haunted conference.** He has gone back every year since. It is the conference Beth knew him as attending. This year's Ignite — Chapter 1 — is when something starts to come undone.

**How he carries it:** He doesn't talk about it. The crew knows, because Callum was the one who flew to Austin and slept on his couch for a week. They don't bring it up. Hal will go months functioning, then drink alone in a hotel room until he can't feel his face. He runs to outpace it. He works to outpace it.

**Tells planted in early chapters:**
- Hums a melody when stressed — his mother's lullaby, in Old Norse, though he thinks he made it up.
- Doodles a single rune (*othala*, "homeland") on every notebook page.
- Murmurs a phrase in his sleep that Beth once asked him about. He told her it was nothing. He didn't know it was a name.
- Carries Maya's favorite hair tie in his wallet.
- Wears his wedding ring on a chain under his shirt.

**Physical:** 6'1", broad-shouldered, salt-and-pepper hair starting to silver at the temples, beard kept short. Crow's-feet from squinting at terminals and from smiling more than he does now. Old scar across his right forearm — childhood farm accident, supposedly. (Older than that.) Strong, but the kind of strong that comes from rage-running on the Town Lake trail at 5 a.m.

**Voice & personality:** Dry, observational, surprisingly warm when he opens up. Listens more than he talks. Funny in a deadpan way. People trust him within five minutes.

### Past Life — Halvard

19 winters at the working. Heir to Eirik Greybeard. Heir to the keeper's duty he didn't yet know existed. Promised to Sigrún. The seer's working caught him with extra protection his father bled into him at the strand — which is why his memories are buried deepest.

### Hal's Arc — Across the Series

- **Book 1:** A grieving man rediscovers that he is loved, that he was loved, and that he has a duty he did not ask for. Ends Book 1 as the new Keeper of Eikvik's binding.
- **Books 2-5:** Slowly learns what holding a keeper's vow actually means. Travels to other bloodlines as a visitor. Becomes a mentor to keepers younger than himself. Marries Erin quietly at Callanish in Book 4. **Sees Beth and Maya in Book 5** in a place between worlds at the top of a sacred Andean peak — Beth blesses Erin in the vision; Maya asks a small question only a daughter would ask; Hal comes down able to grieve forward instead of backward.
- **Book 6:** Whatever the final cost of the series is, it falls on him and Erin together.

## Erin Sigrid Halvorsen — *Sigrún, apprentice of the völva*

> *"She's been having the dream her whole life. She just thought it was a dream."*

### Modern Life

**Full name:** Erin Sigrid Halvorsen *(she gives Hal her middle name late one night in a Berlin hotel bar, and watches his face do something she does not yet understand)*
**Age:** 38
**Lives:** Zurich, Switzerland. A small flat in Wiedikon with too many books.
**Origin:** Norwegian mother (Astrid, professor of computational linguistics at ETH Zurich, deceased five years ago — cancer). Swiss-German father (Christoph, retired civil engineer, lives in Bern). Raised in Zurich, summers with her grandmother in Bergen. Speaks German, English, Norwegian, French, and reads enough Italian to be dangerous.
**Work:** PhD in applied cryptography from **ETH Zurich.** Principal researcher at a Zurich-based security firm, with academic affiliations. Speaks at Black Hat, DEF CON, RSA, and the security tracks at Microsoft Ignite, KubeCon, and Experts Live EU. **Not a Microsoft MVP** — the academic outsider whose research keeps putting her in the same rooms as Hal and the crew.

**Personality:** Brilliant, sharp-tongued, gets visibly impatient with people who can't keep up. Bad at small talk. Luminous when she's talking about her work. Sarcastic in the way that only really lonely smart people are. Has a laugh she's almost embarrassed by.

**The dream.** She has had it her entire life. A man's face she does not recognize. A bonfire on a strand. Her own voice saying *find me.*

**Tells:**
- Silver pendant under her shirts: a rune she does not know the meaning of, given to her by her Norwegian grandmother on her thirteenth birthday with no explanation.
- Cannot stand the smell of woodsmoke without crying.
- Has, twice in her life, finished sentences in Old Norse without realizing.
- Has never been seriously in love.

**Physical:** 5'7", lean from running (sub-three-hour Zurich Marathon finisher). Dark hair usually in a messy bun she's just shoved a pencil into. Glasses she keeps meaning to update. Pale, freckled across the nose. Striking rather than conventionally pretty.

### Past Life — Sigrún

Apprentice to the village *völva*. 18 winters at the working. Promised to Halvard. Her training imprinted the soul, not the mind.

### Erin's Arc — Across the Series

- **Book 1:** Finds Hal. Recovers the *völva*'s training in fragments and then in full. Transcribes Callum's death-chant — which becomes the working they need. Sits on Maya's bedroom floor with Hal at 3 a.m. Accepts the keeper's vow beside him at Callanish.
- **Books 2-5:** Becomes the council's lore-keeper — the one who learns each new bloodline's verses and teaches each new keeper their own forgotten language. **In Book 5, before Hal goes up the mountain to look for Beth and Maya, Erin is the one who tells him to.** *"Look for Beth."* She already knows.
- **Book 6:** Final cost falls on her and Hal together.

## Jan Bjornstad — *Jorund, master shipwright of Eikvik*

> *"If you have a problem, ask Jan. If Jan has a problem, the problem does not know what's coming."*

### Modern Life

**Full name:** Jan Henrik Bjornstad
**Age:** 47
**Lives:** Trondheim, Norway. Wife **Kristine** (architect), kids Magnus (16) and Liv (14).
**Work:** Senior cloud engineer for a Scandinavian energy company. **Azure MVP for eleven years.**
**Origin:** Trained as a finish carpenter before pivoting to IT in his late twenties. Still builds furniture in his garage on weekends.

**Personality:** Dry as a bone. Patient. Unflappable. The steady center of the friend group.

**Physical:** 6'3", lean, weather-faced. Hands that have worked wood for thirty years. The same brand of flannel shirt in seven colors. A beard slowly going Viking on him.

**Tells:**
- His hands know how to do things his mind has never been taught.
- He has built a small wooden boat in his garage over the last six years and cannot say why he started it.

### Past Life — Jorund

Master shipwright of Eikvik. 31 winters at the working. Wife and youngest child did *not* survive — only his eldest son was caught in the spell at the last moment. Jan recovers this in Part Two and nearly breaks. **Kristine, his modern wife, holds him through it without understanding what is happening to him. That is one of the quiet love stories of the book.**

### Jan's Arc — Across the Series

The boat in his garage becomes a literal vessel the council uses in later books. **His son Magnus begins to manifest gifts in Book 3 or 4 — the first sign that the bloodlines continue to produce keepers.** Jan is torn about this. He had hoped to spare his son.

## Callum MacLeod — *Kjartan, warrior-skald of the Hebrides — the Lost One*

> *"He fought, and he sang the dead home."*

### Modern Life

**Full name:** Callum Angus MacLeod
**Age:** 39
**Lives:** Edinburgh. Spent every childhood summer at his grandparents' croft on the Isle of Lewis, three hours from Callanish.
**Work:** Independent IT consultant. **Microsoft 365 MVP for seven years.**

**Marital status — clarification:** Callum had one brief disastrous marriage at 26. **Hal flew in from Austin to be his best man at Callum's wedding.** Callum was divorced two years later, at 28, and has not seriously dated since. In the years following Callum's divorce, **Hal has visited Edinburgh many times** — long weekends, holidays, Hogmanay one year. The friendship runs deep in both directions: Callum was the one who flew to Austin and stayed on Hal's couch for a week after Beth and Maya died. *(Hal himself has never been divorced — he is widowed.)*

**Personality:** The heart of the friend group. Loud, profane, generous. Drinks too much. Sings karaoke without irony. Plays guitar — actually well. Secretly writes poetry he has never shown anyone. Cries at movies. Will fight a man for any of his friends without thinking twice.

**Physical:** 5'10", barrel-chested, ginger going strawberry-blond, full beard. Tattoos he's collected over the years — a few of them, when he sees them in mirrors during the story, will have been runes all along.

**Tells:**
- The song he hums when he's drunk is in a language he insists he doesn't speak. Erin recognizes it before he does.
- He has been to Callanish a hundred times. He always cries at the stones. He doesn't know why.
- He gets into bar fights only at conferences.

### Past Life — Kjartan

Born in the Hebrides. Married into Eikvik. 28 winters at the working. Wife **Una** and infant son back in the Hebrides at the time — both survived. **His bloodline continues in modern Scotland.**

### His Arc — *the Lost One*

Dies in Chapter 17. Verses on his lips. Erin transcribes the chant; in Chapter 20 it turns out to be the working they need.

**Across the series:** Carried in every book that follows. His verses. His grandmother on Lewis (recurring until she dies offscreen between later books). **A modern Hebridean descendant** — Callum and Una's bloodline — becomes a quiet recurring presence from Book 3 onward.

## Stefan Vogel — *Asbjorn the Smith — the Skeptic*

> *"You can't engineer your way out of grief. He'll figure that out about a chapter too late."*

### Modern Life

**Full name:** Stefan Vogel
**Age:** 49
**Lives:** Hamburg, Germany. Industrial loft he renovated himself.
**Work:** Senior systems engineer at a German enterprise IT firm. **Windows MVP for thirteen years** — one of the longest-running MVPs in the inner circle.
**Origin:** Born in Hannover. Spent every weekend of his childhood in his grandfather's metal workshop.
**Personal:** Long-term partner **Maja** (structural engineer). Two grown daughters from his first marriage who he sees regularly.

**Personality:** Engineer's engineer. Allergic to vague language. Believes in things he can measure. Refuses to believe in anything he can't.

**Physical:** Tall, broad, going slightly soft. Shaves his head. Square hands. Always carries a small Leatherman in his pocket.

**Tells:**
- The only person in the inner circle who has never had the dream.
- Noticed Brand at conferences years before anyone else and dismissed it as paranoia. Has not mentioned this. Intends never to mention it.
- His grandfather's last words, on his deathbed in 1997, were in a language Stefan didn't recognize.

### Past Life — Asbjorn

34 winters at the working. **Smith of Eikvik.** Built half the rune-stones the seer used in the working. Wife had died of fever the previous winter; no children of his own, but had fostered two boys whose father had died at sea — both caught in the working with him.

### His Arc — *the Skeptic Turns*

Refuses to believe through Chapters 9–14. Stays in the group because he loves them, not because he believes them.

**Chapter 15:** Alone in his Hamburg workshop, welding a piece of metal that turns out to be the exact shape of a rune he has never seen before, his grandfather's deathbed words come back to him in his own voice. He calls Hal in the middle of the night and says, *"Halvard. I need you to tell me what is happening to me."*

The first time anyone in the modern story has called Hal by his old name aloud, except for Erin in dreams.

### Across the Series

**Stefan leads Book Four** — the binding under industrial Hamburg, bound by his ancestors at the working of a forge during the early medieval period. His grandfather's deathbed words finally make full sense. His crisis of faith in Book Four is the central conflict of that book; he has to learn that doubt is not the opposite of being a keeper.

## Brand Wolfsbane — *the man whose name no one can quite remember*

### Past Life

Sorcerer-jarl from up the fjord. **An operative of an older figure** — see the seed line in Ch 23 below. Wanted what was beneath the land for his master. Caught in the working's outer edge when Eirik struck him down at the strand.

### Modern Existence

Goes by **"Wes"** — full name **Wesley** on the badge, though nobody who sees the badge remembers it. **Sales engineer for a third-tier ISV that makes hybrid-cloud backup software** — the kind of vendor with a small booth in the back corner of every Microsoft conference and almost no actual customers. Exactly the badge nobody looks at twice. He has been at every event the crew has attended for nine years. He has nodded to Hal in coffee lines.

**His tell:** Look directly at him and you can see him. Look away and his name slips. Try to describe him the next day and you produce a bland composite of every man at every conference.

### What He Has Done

**Brand engineered the accident that killed Beth and Maya.** Hal learns this in Chapter 16. Grief becomes rage. The frame inverts.

### What He Has Become

In any conference room he is the most confident man present. Loud opinions on roadmaps. Smooth name-drops that may or may not be real. The kind of man who says *actually* a lot and believes he is the only one doing useful work in whatever conversation he is in. A mildly exhausting presence — the sort that makes people look away first, not remember him afterward. The mask fits well because he has worn it for years.

**What is underneath the mask:** Less than human and more than a man. His face does not stay still in mirrors. He has not loved anyone in seven hundred years and has forgotten what the word means. He believes Hal's village stole his future, and he intends to take Hal's future from him in payment, twice over — once with Beth and Maya, once with Erin. **He will not get the second one. But he will come close.**

### The Crossing

Brand was caught in the outer edge of the seer's working at the moment Eirik struck him down on the strand. The working stripped names from the keepers as protection — but Brand was never one of the keepers, so it took nothing from him. He carried his name, his memory, and his vow through intact.

His master — the Hungering Earth, the thing the village had died to bind — sustained him across the crossing, or across whatever distance lay between the strand and here. He did not age. He did not sleep in any sense a human would understand. He stepped through cold air and on the other side the world was strange and the sky was wrong. He does not know how far he traveled or in which direction. He knows only that his master held him, and that when he arrived this world was waiting, and Hal was in it. He found the conference circuit within a decade. He has been there ever since, because that is where Hal is.

### His End — Ch 23 — *with the Seed Line*

Brand is unmade at Callanish when the working completes. **But before he goes, he speaks once more — a single line, almost an aside, possibly addressed to Hal and possibly addressed to no one in particular:**

> ***"He warned me you'd come at it like this."***

A look at Hal that lasts a beat too long. Then he is gone.

Hal does not register what was said in the moment. Neither does the reader. The line is the **planted seed for the series-level antagonist** — the Old One who was Brand's master and who has been working across centuries against the keepers worldwide. *(See series outline.)*

## The Wider Circle

### Mel Carter — Sydney
**41. DevOps consultant, Azure MVP** (six years). *Not* a villager.
Australian, second-generation Greek-Australian. Loud, profane, hilarious. Mother of two. **Loyalty rivals Callum's.** Comedy duo with Callum at every Ignite for years. **Across the series:** stays loyal across all six books. Becomes one of the council's most trusted operational minds — the one who figures out logistics in places the keepers have never been.

### Hamish Reid — Auckland
**44. Infrastructure consultant, M365 MVP** (eight years). *Not* a villager.
New Zealander, soft-spoken, dryly funny. The one who quietly picks up the bar tab. Often paired with Mel — both fly twenty-plus hours. **Across the series:** stays. By Book 4 or 5 he is the one the others trust to make decisions under fire — partly because he has nothing to prove.

### Annika de Vries — Amsterdam
**36. IAM specialist, Microsoft Security MVP** (four years). ***Villager.***
Dutch, tall, thoughtful, asks the question everyone else was afraid to ask. Reads scientific papers for fun. Married to a marine biologist. Past-life: a *bondi-woman* of Eikvik, one of Sigrún's older friends. **Erin recognizes her almost on sight when memories begin to surface.** **Across the series:** her Amsterdam home becomes the council's de facto European hub from Book 2 onward. Possibly leads Book 5.

### Mihai Stancu — Toronto *(via Bucharest)*
**38. Security consultant, Azure MVP** (three years). *Not* a villager.
Born in Bucharest, emigrated to Canada in his early twenties. Speaks five languages. Comic-relief energy masking a serious mind. Becomes one of the strongest non-villager allies in Part Three. **Across the series — Mihai leads Book Three** (Romania). The book where the comic-relief character becomes the man whose work matters most. His Bucharest childhood, his Orthodox grandmother who insisted on certain rituals, his five languages — these turn out to be a *different* kind of inheritance.

### Tony Reyes — Atlanta
**40. Principal Cloud Architect, Azure MVP** (seven years). ***Villager.***
Cuban-American, gregarious, the connector. Part-time foster parent with his husband Marco. Past-life: a trader from Vikvágr who married into Eikvik a few years before the working. **Across the series — Tony bridges Book Five** (the Andes). His Spanish opens the door; his nature as a connector opens the people. One of his and Marco's foster children, now grown, comes along.

### Sarah Wexler — Bay Area
**35. Microsoft program manager, M365 MVP** (two years — newest). *Not* a villager.
Sharp, ambitious, the youngest in the inner circle. Got pulled in three years ago when Hal mentored her at the MVP Summit. Family by adoption, not blood. **Across the series:** her arc is the most quietly powerful non-villager arc — *she is the one who makes the council ask the real question across all six books: who deserves to be a keeper? The ones born to it, or the ones who chose it?* **In Book Four she is the first to learn Marcus's fate.** The way she carries that across Books 5 and 6 is what gives her question its answer.

### Marcus Chen — Chicago
**42. DevOps lead. Not an MVP.** *Not* a villager.
The conference friend who isn't actually in the MVP world but somehow ended up in their orbit. Met Tony at MMS MOA seven years ago. Important social glue — the reminder that not everyone in this story is some ancient soul.

**Across the series — Marcus has a planned arc that ends in Book Four.** Because he is the *normal-life thread* the council still has, his loss is meant to land harder than any of the keeper-deaths. The arc:

- **Book One:** Already in the road trip to the Hebrides, perimeter at Callanish, Callum's funeral. **At Callanish (Ch 22), he holds the perimeter outside the circle — this is the moment he first observes what it costs to hold from outside. Seed for Book Four.**
- **Book Two (Japan):** Doesn't go. Chicago DevOps guy with a regular life. Present on the group chat. **One key scene: flies to Austin between Hal's Japan trips just to make sure Hal is eating.** That scene is the seed for everything Marcus does later.
- **Book Three (Romania):** Goes. Mihai brings the crew home. Marcus sees keepers do something openly for the first time. Almost quits. Doesn't. The chapter where he sits in a Bucharest hotel room at 2 a.m. on the phone with his mother in Chicago, not telling her anything but needing to hear her voice, is a key chapter for him.
- **Book Four (Hamburg):** Asks to come. Stefan tries to talk him out of it. Comes anyway. **The climactic working needs an anchor outside the circle.** If a keeper holds that position, they are trapped in the binding forever — a living death. If a non-keeper does it, they don't have the soul-stuff to survive contact with the working. They die. **Marcus volunteers because he is the only one who *can*.** Dying words, small and not a speech: ***"Tell my mom I wasn't scared."*** Sarah Wexler finds out first, in her POV chapter.

The thematic point: the keepers were *born* to this. Marcus *chose* it. That is the answer to Sarah's question.

After Book Four, the lifted-glass moments in every subsequent book are for both Callum and Marcus.
